How shapes work — 20-second tour
The shape walks a pool of notes — the chord tones, or the chord scale. Row 1 is home; rows above step up the pool, rows below dip under it. Each column is one step in time. After the shape plays once, Travel slides it through the pool and it plays again — the faint dots show where it goes next. Approach notes reach outside the pool: ‹/› a half step below/above the tone, «/» a scale step below/above. Several shapes (tabs A, B, …) rotate bar by bar. Everything here is the same thing as the text box — 1 3 2 4, <1 1, 1 2 3 4 ; scale: 1 2 3 — whichever is faster for you. Notes from can also alternate bar by bar between chord tones and the scale (the slider sets the ratio, e.g. 2 chord bars : 1 scale bar), and the Scale picker swaps in pentatonic, blues, harmonic- or melodic-minor pools. Each bar-shape can carry its own pool and travel — the little selects above the grid, or in text: 1 2 3 4 @down1 ; scale: 1 3 2 4 @up2 makes shape A fall by 1 while shape B climbs by 2. A bar's own override (in Bars below) still beats the shape's tag.
